I love backpacking. I took my first foray into a wild place with my dad when I was only 11 years old, and I've been hooked ever since! After a few autumns of short trips in the southern Appalachian mountains of Georgia, North Carolina, and Tennessee, I decided to go on my first long backpacking trip right after I graduated high school. I was incredibly fit after my senior-year track season, and ready to head into the high sierra in central California and hike a little over 200 miles on "America's most beautify hike," the John Muir Trail. Check out these photos from my trip!

On My Way!

Here I am at the Happy Isles trailhead, where my dad photographed me prior to my departure. He walked with me up the trail a few miles, but then I was on my own in the backcountry for the first time.

Approaching Halfdome

A false color image of Halfdome, seen from the JMT leaving Yosemite valley.

Goodbye, Halfdome...

This was my last view of Halfdome, seen from atop Cloud's Rest. To the right of Halfdome is Yosemite Valley, where I left my dad the day before. An hour before this picture was taken I came within a meter of a rattlesnake before becoming aware of its presence. Luckily for me, the snake had just caught lunch: a big mouse filled its mouth. That is still the most scary experience I've had in the backcountry.

Reunited!

This picture was taken in Lyell Canyon, just a few hours outside of Tuolomne Meadows. My dad is down there taking in the beauty and refilling our water. This was one of our most favorite places along the trail: 5 miles of flat walking through this canyon with wildlife everywhere and an awesome stream for dipping.

Swimming Hole

I jumped off of this rock into the stream about a dozen times. This was really close to Road's End in Sequioa National Park, where I got my last food resupply for the final leg of my hike.

Finished!

Here I am on the top of Mt. Whitney! Although I completed the official JMT route, I still had 13 miles to walk down from the summit to the valley below, which was not easy.

Halfdome on the morning of my ascent.

I climbed Halfdome on the first morning I woke up in camp. I camped near the base of the Halfdome spur trail, and woke up early to beat the day hikers. This is what the mountain looked like as I approached! I would hate to climb it with people filling that rope ladder, that seems like it would be dangerous and just downright unpleasant. I had a great time roaming the top with only a few other people.

Nose Bleed in the Meadow

The dry mountain air (and maybe the altitude too) gave me a nose bleed in this meadow. It wasn't a big deal though, I controlled the bleeding with a glove I had and washed up in a nearby stream. How could one be upset in a meadow like this?! This photo was taken on the 3rd or 4th day of my trip, just a day or two before I reached Tuolomne Meadows and met up to hike with my dad for the next week.

Goodbye, Yosemite...

As we approached the end of Lyell Canyon we looked up at this monster climb to Donahue Pass and the border of Yosemite National Park. We were sad to leave the canyon, but excited to enter the high country.

Mystery Lake

I can't remember what lake this was. I think it might be Rae Lakes. Wherever it is, I hope I return some day!

Mt. Whitney From Behind

This was my view of the Mt. Whitney Massif as I approached it. I think this photo was taken at Timberline Lake. Later this day I stopped to wait and summit the mountain the following moring at sunrise, but I ended up meeting two awesome guys from San Fransisco and climbing most of the mountain that night. We spent a windy and chilly night high on the mountain, setting up our sleeping bags right in the middle of the trail, and summitted the following morning.
