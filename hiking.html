My Ultralight Thru-hiking Philosophy

After backpacking a decent amount, I've developed a style and preference for how I like to travel through wild places and what I like to take with me. A lot of this is inspired by the ultralight backpacking community: minimize the weight, amount, and complexity of your things without compromising safety. But a lot of people tend to think that this means sacrificing comfort so you can travel faster and farther. Carrying lighter and less gear definitely does allow for this kind of travel, but I've learned that, for me, it makes all kinds of walking just more enjoyable. Even if I only walk 10 miles in a day, I have more fun and my body feels better with less stuff. I feel better at camp and can enjoy my surroundings more. I'm "closer to nature" because I'm not distracted by the complexity or amount of the human-made things I'm carrying. And I have fewer items to forget at camp, or to worry about packing up in the morning. Overall, I've found that the ultralight ideals make all of my backcountry excursions more fulfilling and pleasant. At the same time, though, I don't spend huge amounts of money just to try to reach some arbitrary maximum base-weight, and I don't fail to bring things that I want or need because it will make my pack lighter; instead, I try to want and need fewer things.
